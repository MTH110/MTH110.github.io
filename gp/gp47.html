Guided Practice 4.7: Graphing Quadratics from Standard Form

Overview

We've seen that we can extract a lot of information about a quadratic function if it's given to us in vertex form. At a glance, we can tell information about whether the parabola opens up or down, the location of the vertex, and information about the axis of symmetry. But what if we are working with a quadratic that isn't in vertex form but in standard form, that is in the form \(f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c\)? The information we want to apply from such a function may not be obvious, but in this lesson we will find some ways to quickly determine the information we need.

Learning objectives

Basic objectives: Each student is responsible for gaining proficiency with each of these tasks prior to engaging in class discussions, through the use of the learning resources (below) and through the working of exercises (also below).

  • Given the formula for a quadratic in standard form:
    • Find the vertical intercepts.
    • Find the horizontal intercepts.
    • State the coordinates of the vertex.
    • Determine whether the parabola opens up or down.
    • Identify the axis of symmetry and write it as an equation.

Advanced objectives: The following objectives are the subject of class discussion and further work; they should be mastered by each student during and following class discussions.

  • Find the domain and range of a quadratic.
  • Given the standard form of a quadratic, sketch the parabola by hand.
  • Given a quadratic that models a real-life situation, sketch a graph by hand, determine its characteristics (vertex, axis of symmetry, domain, range, etc.) and interpret the meanings of these characteristics in context.
